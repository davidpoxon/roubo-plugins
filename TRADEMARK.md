# Roubo Trademark Policy

The name **Roubo** and the Roubo logomark (collectively, the "Roubo Marks") are
trademarks of David Poxon. This policy explains what you may and may not do
with them.

Roubo's source code is released under the Apache License, Version 2.0. Section
6 of that licence is explicit: the licence does **not** grant rights to use the
Roubo Marks. The marks are governed by this policy instead.

## Summary

You may use the Roubo Marks to refer to Roubo factually: to say your project
works with Roubo, is based on Roubo, or to discuss Roubo in writing. You may
not use the Roubo Marks to brand your own software, your own services, or
modified versions of Roubo, nor in any way that suggests endorsement.

## What you may do

- Refer to Roubo by name in documentation, articles, talks, blog posts, and
  social media.
- State that your project is compatible with, integrates with, or is built on
  top of Roubo, provided the statement is accurate.
- State that your fork is "based on Roubo" or "derived from Roubo" in prose,
  provided your fork has its own distinct name (see below).
- Use the unmodified Roubo logomark to link back to the official Roubo
  repository or website.

## What you may not do

Except where you have received written permission from David Poxon (see
[Express written permission](#express-written-permission) below), you may
not:

- Distribute a modified version of Roubo under the name "Roubo" or under any
  name that includes "Roubo" as a component (for example: "Roubo Pro",
  "RouboX", "OpenRoubo", "Roubo Plus", "Roubo Community Edition").
- Use the Roubo Marks as the name of your product, service, company, domain,
  package, plugin, or extension.
- Modify the Roubo logomark, or use it in a way that suggests it represents
  your product or organisation rather than Roubo itself.
- Use the Roubo Marks in a manner that suggests endorsement, sponsorship, or
  affiliation by the Roubo project or by David Poxon.
- Register the Roubo Marks (or confusingly similar marks) as trademarks,
  domain names, or social media handles.

## Forks

You are free to fork Roubo under the Apache 2.0 licence. Unless you have
written permission from David Poxon, a fork must use a distinct name, one
that does not include "Roubo" and is not confusingly similar. You may state
in your fork's documentation that it is based on Roubo, provided that
statement is accurate and not the primary brand.

If you redistribute Roubo unmodified, you may retain the Roubo name and marks
as required to comply with the Apache 2.0 licence's attribution conditions.

## Permitted descriptive uses

The following phrasings are permitted because they describe a factual
relationship rather than claiming the Roubo name as your own:

- "Built on Roubo"
- "Compatible with Roubo"
- "Roubo plugin for X"
- "Fork of Roubo"
- "Inspired by Roubo"

The following phrasings are not permitted because they adopt the Roubo name
as a product identifier:

- "Roubo Enterprise"
- "Roubo for Teams"
- "MyCompany Roubo"
- "Roubo by X"

## Express written permission

Any restriction in this policy can be waived, in whole or in part, by
written permission from David Poxon. Only David Poxon can grant such
permission.

To request permission, email <trademark@roubo.dev> with:

- A description of how you'd like to use the Roubo Marks.
- The scope of the proposed use: where it will appear, for how long, and
  in what context.
- Any other information that would help in making the decision.

If permission is granted, the response will state the terms in writing. A
grant is limited to exactly what the response describes. A grant for one
use does not extend to others. Permission may be revoked in writing if
circumstances change; in that case, you will be given a reasonable period
to wind down the affected use.

Verbal approvals, informal social-media replies, or inferred consent do
not count as written permission for the purposes of this policy. If
you're not sure whether you have permission, you don't.
